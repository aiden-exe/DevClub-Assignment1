Do's and Don'ts of Covid Pandemic

The world has halted in many areas due to the global pandemic caused by the COVID-19 virus.
The disease has spread all around the globe and is highly contagious. Due to this, it is our duty
to follow safety measures so that we don't get the disease. This ensures the safety of both
ourselves, and those around us.

Do your bit to save the world!

Measures

Wear a mask! Make wearing a mask a normal part of being around other people. The appropriate use, storage and cleaning or disposal of masks are essential to make them as effective as possible.

Keep social Distancing! If you are near another person, make sure to maintain a minimum of 2 meter or 6 feet distance from them.

Wash your hands regularly! Washing is the best way to kill virses on your hands. If you cannot wash your hands somewhere, use an alcohol based sanitizer.

FAQs

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a disease caused by a virus called SARS-CoV-2. Most people with COVID-19 have mild symptoms, but some people can become severely ill. Although most people with COVID-19 get better within weeks of illness, some people experience post-COVID conditions. Post-COVID conditions are a wide range of new, returning or ongoing health problems people can experience more than four weeks after first being affected with the virus that causes COVID-19. Older people and those who have certain underlying medical conditions are more likely to get severly ill from COVID-19. Vaccines against COVID-19 are safe and effective.

Should I use soap and water or hand sanitizer to protect myself?

Handwashing is one of the best ways to protect yourself and your close ones from getting sick. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing; going to the bathroom; and before eating or preparing food. If soap and water are not readily available, use an alcohol based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.

What are the symptoms and complications that COVID-19 can cause?

People with COVID-19 have reported a wide range of symptoms - from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. If you have fever, cough, or other symptoms, you might have COVID-19.
